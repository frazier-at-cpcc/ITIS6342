Part One: Case Narrative and Assignment

Case Narrative

Background

MedTech Solutions is a mid-sized healthcare technology company with 450 employees that provides electronic health record (EHR) systems to regional hospital networks. The company has been in business for 12 years and has a solid reputation for reliability, though competitors have begun offering more modern, cloud-based solutions.

The Project

Six months ago, MedTech’s executive team approved "Project DataFlow"—an ambitious initiative to migrate their legacy on-premises EHR system to a cloud-based microservices architecture. The project was given an 18-month timeline and a $4.2 million budget. Key drivers included:

  • Increasing customer demands for mobile access and real-time analytics

  • Rising maintenance costs for legacy infrastructure (currently $1.8M annually)

  • Competitive pressure from newer market entrants

  • Technical debt accumulated over a decade of incremental updates

The Project Team

Sarah Chen (10 years PM experience, PMP certified, but new to MedTech)

Technical Lead

Marcus Williams (15-year MedTech veteran, deep legacy system knowledge)

Development Team

12 developers (mix of legacy experts and new cloud hires)

Operations Team

Current Situation (Month 6)

You have been brought in as an external consultant. The project sponsor, CTO David Okonkwo, has expressed serious concerns. Here is what your initial assessment reveals:

Scope Issues

  • The original project charter was approved with a two-paragraph scope statement

  • No formal Work Breakdown Structure was ever created

  • Stakeholders continue to request new features; 23 change requests have been logged, 18 were approved without formal impact analysis

  • The development team is unsure which requirements are "must-have" vs. "nice-to-have"

  • Two major hospital clients have been promised custom integrations that were never in the original scope

Schedule Issues

  • The project is using a Gantt chart created in month 1 that has not been updated

  • No critical path analysis was performed

  • Dependencies between the legacy team and cloud team are causing constant delays

  • Three developers have been pulled onto emergency maintenance work for existing clients an average of 15 hours per week

  • Sprint planning meetings are routinely skipped due to "urgent priorities"

Cost Issues

  • The project has spent $2.1 million (50% of budget) but completed only approximately 25% of planned functionality

  • No earned value metrics are being tracked

  • Cloud infrastructure costs are running 40% higher than estimated

  • Two additional contractors were hired without budget approval

Quality Issues

  • No quality management plan exists

  • Testing is done ad-hoc, usually the day before releases

  • The QA team reports finding critical defects that were marked as "resolved" but never actually fixed

  • Technical debt from the legacy system is being replicated in the new architecture

  • Code reviews are inconsistent—some modules have had thorough reviews, others none

Team Dynamics

  • Marcus (Technical Lead) and Sarah (PM) have conflicting visions for the architecture

  • Legacy developers feel marginalized and worry about job security

  • Cloud developers are frustrated by what they perceive as "outdated thinking"

  • Operations was not included in planning and now has concerns about deployment and monitoring

  • The QA team feels ignored when they raise defect concerns

Communication Issues

  • Status reports are sent monthly but contain only high-level summaries

  • Stakeholders receive different information depending on who they ask

  • No project dashboard or centralized information repository exists

  • The executive sponsor receives optimistic updates while the team discusses serious problems

Document J: Compliance and Regulatory Context

MedTech Solutions operates in a regulated healthcare environment. These constraints affect project decisions and timelines.

Relevant Regulatory Requirements

HIPAA Security Rule: All systems handling protected health information (PHI) must implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards. Cloud migration requires updated Business Associate Agreements with all cloud vendors and documented security controls.

State Reporting Mandates: Several client states require standardized electronic reporting formats by specific deadlines:

  • North Carolina: July 1, 2024 — HL7 FHIR-based quality reporting

  • Virginia: October 1, 2024 — Updated immunization registry format

  • Georgia: January 1, 2025 — Prescription drug monitoring integration

Meaningful Use / Promoting Interoperability: Clients face financial penalties if their EHR systems don’t meet federal interoperability standards. MedTech’s current certification expires in 14 months and must be renewed with the new platform.

Scheduled Audit

MedTech’s annual HIPAA security audit is scheduled for Month 10 (four months from current assessment). The audit scope includes any systems handling PHI, which will include DataFlow components if any are in production use by that date.

The Four Types of Work (Phoenix Project Reference)

Type Description

Business Projects

Work that directly supports business initiatives and has clear business sponsorship

IT Projects

Infrastructure and technology improvements initiated by IT

Changes

Updates to existing systems, often generated from business or IT projects

Unplanned Work

Recovery work, firefighting, emergency fixes — work that displaces planned work

The Three Ways (Phoenix Project Reference)

Way Principle Application

First Way

Flow

Optimize the flow of work from left to right (development to operations to customer). Make work visible, reduce batch sizes, reduce handoffs, identify and elevate constraints.

Second Way

Feedback

Create fast feedback loops from right to left. Amplify feedback to prevent problems from progressing downstream. Enable faster detection and recovery.

Third Way

Continuous Learning

Create a culture of experimentation and learning from failure. Allocate time for improvement. Share knowledge across the organization.
